# GBP, NAP, Local Pack Audit

**Purpose:** Audit Google Business Profile completeness, Name/Address/Phone consistency across the web, and local pack ranking performance — the three things that actually move local rankings.

**When to run this:** Onboarding any client with a physical location or service area, and quarterly thereafter. Directly relevant to vertical-specific local plays (e.g., Perth/Sydney Recruitment, Mining, Energy, Aged Care work).

**Inputs needed:**
- GBP listing access or export
- List of known directory/citation listings (or willingness to discover via search)
- Target local pack queries + service area

**Process:**
1. Audit GBP completeness: category accuracy (primary + secondary), hours, services/products list, photos (recency and volume), description, and whether posts are being published regularly.
2. Pull NAP citations across major directories and data aggregators — flag any inconsistency in name formatting, address formatting, or phone number; even small inconsistencies (Street vs St.) measurably hurt local trust signals.
3. Check review velocity and response rate — a profile with old reviews and no owner responses underperforms a profile with steady recent reviews and active responses, independent of star rating.
4. Run target queries from multiple geo-grid points across the service area (not just from the business address) to see actual local pack visibility variance by location.
5. Compare against top 3 local pack competitors on the same completeness/review/citation dimensions to find the specific gap, not just "we're not ranking."

**Output format:**
`GBP completeness score | NAP consistency issues found (with sources) | Review velocity (last 90 days) vs top competitor | Geo-grid visibility map | Top 3 fixes ranked by effort`.

**Quality bar:** A geo-grid check from a single point (usually the office) overstates visibility — always check from multiple points across the actual service area before reporting a ranking position.
